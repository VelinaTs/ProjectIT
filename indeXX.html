Soccer

Soccer, also known as football outside North America, is a sport played between two teams of eleven players with a spherical ball. It is the world's most popular sport. The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end.

History

The history of soccer can be traced back over 2,000 years to ancient China, Greece, Rome, and parts of Central America. However, the modern game as we know it today began in England in the 19th century.

Basketball

Basketball is a team sport in which two teams, most commonly of five players each, opposing one another on a rectangular court, compete with the primary objective of shooting a basketball through the defender's hoop.

History

Basketball was invented in December 1891 by Canadian James Naismith in Springfield, Massachusetts, USA. He developed the sport as a way to keep his students active indoors during the cold winters.

Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent or between two teams of two players each. Each player uses a tennis racket that is strung with cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net and into the opponent's court.

History

The modern game of tennis originated in Birmingham, England, in the late 19th century as "lawn tennis". It has its roots in several games played by various European aristocrats from the 12th century onward.
